Understanding Menstruation

Menstruation, or a period, is a natural part of growing up for girls, usually beginning between the ages of 10 and 16, with an average starting age of 12. It involves the shedding of the uterine lining, which typically lasts 3-7 days and occurs once a month. During this time, girls may experience symptoms like cramps, bloating, mood swings, and fatigue. Menstrual cycles vary in length but generally last between 21-35 days.

Menarche

Menarche is the term used for a girl's first menstrual period, marking the beginning of menstruation and the transition into puberty. It typically occurs between ages 10 and 16, with the average age around 12. Menarche signals that a girl's body is capable of reproduction. Menopause

Menopause refers to the natural biological process that marks the end of a woman's menstrual cycles and fertility, typically occurring between the ages of 45 and 55, with the average age around 51. It is diagnosed after a woman has gone 12 consecutive months without a period. Menopause happens when the ovaries stop producing eggs and levels of estrogen and progesterone decline. This leads to the cessation of menstruation and can cause symptoms such as hot flashes, night sweats, mood swings, sleep disturbances, and vaginal dryness.
